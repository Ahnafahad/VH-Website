## Section 1: Financial Statements & Balance Sheet (DU Past Questions)

### Question 1

**Question:**  
Financial position of an organization can be known from:

**Options:**  
A. Statement of cash flows  
B. Balance sheet  
C. Statement of owner's equity  
D. Profit and loss account  
E. Trading account  

**Correct Answer:**  
**B**

**Explanation:**  
The **balance sheet** shows assets, liabilities, and owner’s equity at a specific date, which is exactly the firm’s **financial position**.

**Options:**  
A. Gains and losses  
B. Share premium  
C. Income tax expenses  
D. Sales discount  
E. Non-operating income  

**Correct Answer:**  
**B**

**Explanation:**  
**Share premium** (share capital in excess of par) is part of **equity** and appears in the **balance sheet**, not in the income statement.

### Question 3

**Question:**  
A motor vehicle was purchased for Tk. 8,000 that includes Tk. 36 for petrol. The total payment of Tk. 8,000 was debited to the **motor vehicle account**. What was the effect on the profit and loss account and the balance sheet?

**Options:**  
A. Profit overstated by Tk. 36 and fixed assets overstated by Tk. 36  
B. Profit overstated by Tk. 36 and fixed assets understated by Tk. 36  
C. Profit understated by Tk. 36 and fixed assets overstated by Tk. 36  
D. Profit understated by Tk. 36 and fixed assets understated by Tk. 36  
E. Profit not affected but fixed assets overstated by Tk. 36  

**Correct Answer:**  
**A**

**Explanation:**  
Tk. 36 petrol is a **revenue expense** that should have reduced profit. Instead it was capitalised as part of the asset. So:  
- **Profit is overstated** by Tk. 36 (expense not recorded), and  
- **Fixed assets are overstated** by Tk. 36 (asset includes petrol).

### Question 21

**Question:**  
Machinery was acquired for Tk. 250,000 and now has a book value of Tk. 100,000 and an estimated market value of Tk. 120,000. Replacement cost is Tk. 350,000. At what amount should the machinery be reported in the balance sheet?

### Question 26

**Question:**  
An organization has the following balances:  
Buildings Tk. 20,000; Accounts receivable Tk. 5,000; Cash in hand Tk. 9,000; Bills payable Tk. 10,000; Accounts payable Tk. 6,000; Loan on mortgage Tk. 8,000. What is the owners' equity?

**Options:**  
A. Tk. 18,000  
B. Tk. 25,000  
C. Tk. 17,000  
D. Tk. 10,000  
E. Tk. 15,000  

**Correct Answer:**  
**D**

**Explanation:**  
Total assets = 20,000 + 5,000 + 9,000 = **34,000**  
Total liabilities = 10,000 + 6,000 + 8,000 = **24,000**  
Owners’ equity = Assets − Liabilities = 34,000 − 24,000 = **Tk. 10,000**.

## Section 2: Closing Entries & Post‑Closing Trial Balance (DU Past Questions)

### Question 47

**Question:**  
When a **net loss** has occurred, income summary is:

**Options:**  
A. Debited and drawing is credited  
B. Debited and owner's capital is credited  
C. Credited and owner's capital is debited  
D. Credited and retained earning is debited  
E. Credited and owner's drawing is debited  

**Correct Answer:**  
**C**

**Explanation:**  
With a **net loss**, the Income Summary has a **debit balance**. To close it:  
- **Debit Owner's Capital** (reducing equity), and  
- **Credit Income Summary**.  
So income summary is **credited** and owner’s capital is **debited**.

**Correct Answer:**  
**E**

### Question 54

**Question:**  
If the balance in retained earnings on January 1, 2000 is Tk. 63,000 and the company incurs a **net loss of Tk. 12,000** for the year 2000, the closing entries would include:

**Options:**  
A. A debit to retained earnings of Tk. 63,000
